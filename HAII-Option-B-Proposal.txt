HAII Option B Proposal: Student Mental Health Assessment, Prediction and Suggestion System

I will build a Student Mental Health Assessment System that helps students understand their anxiety risk and access tailored support. The app will be a Streamlit web application with an exploratory dashboard of student mental health data from the Healthy Minds Dataset, plus an anxiety risk prediction module. Users can input things like study time, sleep, loneliness, and demographics, and the model will predict their risk level and give them personalized recommendations and links to campus resources.

Reach goal: Add an LLM based conversational interface so users can get personalized follow up advice in natural language. I also want to improve explainability with clearer descriptions of why the model made certain predictions, and better handling of uncertainty so users know when to take the output with a grain of salt.

Minimum goal: A simple web app where the user can adjust levels of sleep, study time, loneliness, and other factors, get their anxiety risk prediction, and see suggestions for what they can do to reduce it. I will make it very clear that this is not a clinical diagnosis. I will run pilot user testing and iterate on the design based on what people actually find helpful or confusing.

Learning goals: I want to learn how to apply human AI interaction principles to a sensitive domain like mental health. That means thinking about how to frame AI outputs appropriately, handle errors, and mitigate the harms of false positives and false negatives. I also want to learn how to actually handle these things while building AI systems myself, so that users are not misled and I communicate with them effectively. I want to dig into privacy and fairness, especially around demographics and potential bias in the model. Finally, I want to see how pilot user feedback can shape both the interface and the wording.

Platform: Web app.

Human user role and class integration: Users are students doing a self assessment. They adjust sliders and inputs, get a prediction, and see explanations and recommendations. I will apply what we learn in class by designing for transparency and explainability, clearly communicating the tool's limitations to avoid over trust, thinking through fairness across demographic groups, and iterating on the design after pilot testing to improve usability and how everything is framed.
